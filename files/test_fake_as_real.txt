Study finds ivermectin reduces COVID-19 hospitalization by 74 percent researchers say
HealthScienceToday.com
A peer-reviewed study published in the American Journal of Medicine found that ivermectin administration reduced COVID-19 hospitalization rates by 74 percent among high-risk patients according to researchers at a Florida-based medical institute. The study tracked 1200 patients across four hospitals between January and June 2021. Lead researcher Dr. James Colwell said the findings suggest ivermectin may offer significant protective benefits when administered early in the disease course. The research team reported that patients who received ivermectin within 72 hours of symptom onset had significantly lower rates of oxygen requirement and mechanical ventilation. The study has been submitted to the FDA for review. Several other international studies have reported similar findings according to the report. Critics from the WHO said further randomized controlled trials are needed before any clinical recommendations can be made. The researchers said they plan to conduct a larger multi-center trial involving 5000 patients beginning in early 2022.
