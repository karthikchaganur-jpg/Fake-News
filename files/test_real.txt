Federal Reserve raises interest rates by 25 basis points to highest level in 22 years
Reuters
The United States Federal Reserve raised its benchmark interest rate by 25 basis points on Wednesday bringing the federal funds rate to a target range of 5.25 to 5.5 percent the highest level since January 2001. The decision was unanimous among the 11 voting members of the Federal Open Market Committee. Fed Chair Jerome Powell said in a press conference following the announcement that the committee remains committed to returning inflation to its 2 percent target and that further increases remain possible depending on incoming economic data. The Consumer Price Index rose 3.2 percent year over year in June down significantly from the peak of 9.1 percent recorded in June 2022. Economists surveyed by Reuters had widely expected the 25 basis point increase. US stock markets responded positively to the announcement with the S&P 500 rising 0.5 percent following Powell's remarks suggesting the rate hiking cycle may be nearing its end. The Federal Reserve has now raised rates 11 times since March 2022 in its most aggressive tightening campaign in four decades.
